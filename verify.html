What These Numbers Mean

Colour Strength
Measures the crocin content — the compound responsible for saffron's golden colour. The higher the number, the richer the colour in your cooking. Grade I requires a minimum of 190 (ISO 3632-1:2011).
Flavour Depth
Measures picrocrocin — the compound responsible for saffron's distinctive bitter, earthy taste. This is what makes Persian rice taste like Persian rice. Grade I minimum is 70.
Aroma Reading
Measures safranal — the volatile compound responsible for saffron's unmistakeable fragrance. It is the first quality indicator to degrade with poor storage. Grade I range is 20 to 50.
Moisture
Saffron should contain less than 12% moisture. Excess moisture accelerates degradation and can indicate improper storage or adulteration with added water weight.
